Interative Media 1 - RMIT university

LUNI APP

Interactive Experience Reasearch

Luni is a French mobile app publisher and developer who focuses on creating and launching popular ios based apps, primarily grounded in health, fitness, wellness and productivity.

01

FIRST IMPRESSION

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When I landed on the site, the colour palette drew my attention, and after the page had completely loaded, the high-energy, 3D visuals and the oversized typography that followed mouse movement were mesmerizing. But at that time, the interactions were lagging quite often, which was a bit frustrating. The vibrant colour palette and the constant motion of the interface elements created a sense of modern, high-tech minimalism that demanded attention before reading any specific text.

03

MOST TIME SPENT

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging with the "studio" section. The way the site showcases their different products through smooth transitions and high-quality mockups encouraged me to stay on the page longer to see the variety of their portfolio.

04

MOST COMMON ACTION

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action during the interaction was scrolling. Becauses the site utilizes a "scroll-telling" technique where information is revealed through vertical movement and animations are triggered by scrolling down and up, it became the primary way to navigate the content and explore the visuals of the site.

05

PRIMARY GOAL

What is your impression of the intented primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal of the interactive experience appears to be their branding and recruitment. The site is not trying to sell a single product directly, rather, it aims to estabilish Luni as a prestigious, venture-builder to attract top-tier talent and partners who value high-end aesthetics and innocation and they also follow a singular modern-3D-digital cozy aesthetic.

06

COMMUNICATING THE GOAL

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The experience communicates this goal through its design quality, by prioritizing a "premium" feel and showing off their culture and successful app launches, the site as a visual portfolio says, "we focus on high-end products and high-end developvers who make these pruducts, we are a close knit group and we are inviting you to be a part of this group."

07

INTERACTION OVER TIME

What is your impression of how the experience ahould be interacted with over time?

The impression is that this is a "one-time" experience rather than a daily utility. A user would likely spend 5-6 minutes exploring the entire site once to understand the company's identity or for inspirition, and might return only occasionally, perhaps once or twice to check for maybe a new job opening or new app releases.

08

COMMUNICATING TEMPORAL USE

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The site communicates this through its linear narrative structure. The site presents itself as a digital art work; its completeness suggests that once you have navigated the "studio" and "career" sections, you have consumed the usual experience, once that is experienced there are no "dynamic" works or social loops that suggest or make you come back to check check daily. it feels like a finished and polished digital brochure.

09

MEDIA REFERENCES

What other media forms does the experience reference?

The content part of the experience that is the studio, career or Omada, references high-end editorial magazines because of its use of bold, sophisticated yet clean typography, and also portrays luxury automotives or technology showcases through its use of 3D motion and sleek, light-mode aesthetics. It also references soft-aesthetic mobile app interfaces through its "snappy" transitions and icon-heavy layout than text-heavy layout.

10

REFERENCES AND BEHAVIOUR

What does this reference communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with the experience?

These references communicates that I should act as a "observer of quality" or a "guardian". It suggests a slowed-down interaction where I am not rushing to find a piece of data but to appreciate the craft of the website itself.

11

REFERENCES AND FEELING

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The references are designed to make the user feel a sense of aspiration and mesmerization. The sleek, soft aesthetic combined with smooth 3D animation is meant to evoke a feeling of professionalism with a touch of fun and innovation, and exclusivity, making the user feel like they are looking at the future of app development.

12

MOST FRUSTRATING

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction is the "lagging-scroll" or the heavy reliance on heavy animations which leads to slight lag on some devices. This is frustrating because it takes away the user's total control over the website and information retrieval, forcing them to wait for an animation to finish before they can move on to see the next section.

13

MOST SATISFYING

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part is the seamless transition between pages. When clicking a menu item, the way the elements morph or slide out without making the user feel overstimulated, feels inscredibly polished and tactile, providing a high level of 'dopamine" that reinforces the high-quality nature of the brand.
